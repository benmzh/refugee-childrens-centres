Our Projects

Dunkirk

Early in March 2016 a new refugee camp opened in Grande-Synthe, Dunkirk, built to rehouse up to 2500 refugees living in shocking conditions on a boggy wasteland at nearby Basroch. The camp, the first internationally recognised refugee camp in France, was set up as part of a joint project between the medical aid charity Medicins Sans Frontieres (MSF) and the local authority of Grande-Synthe under the leadership of Mayor Damien Careme who could no longer bear to see people living in such terrible conditions. The wooden huts were soon full of families.

There are now about 1000 residents in La Liniere Camp. The vast majority are from the autonomous Kurdish region of Northern Iraq which has suffered greatly under the advance of ISIS/Daesh. There are also several Farsi families and a growing number of families from Afghanistan.

A small group of volunteers in the new camp took over from Dunkirk Kid’s Welfare who had been running a play space in the old camp. Within a short time a wooden semi-permanent school room was donated and built by the Hummingbird Charity and Brighton Shelter Build to give the children a safe, warm place to spend their days and this gradually developed into what is now known as Dunkirk Refugee Children’s Centre. The Children’s Centre has become a place where children can come along as they wish, to find comfort, friendship and fun, to learn a little English and French and to make a start on the road to an education.
